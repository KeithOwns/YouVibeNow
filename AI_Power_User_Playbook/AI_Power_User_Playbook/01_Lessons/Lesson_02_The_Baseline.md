# Lesson 2: Establishing the Baseline (ChatGPT Settings)

To enable Thin Prompting (as discussed in [[Lesson_01_Context_Engineering]]), we need a baseline context. We do this by configuring the core settings in ChatGPT. This turns a generic AI into *your* personalized AI. (See [[02_The_Baseline_Settings]] for the theory).

## Navigating to the Settings
To get started, we need to open the settings menu in your ChatGPT desktop or web app.
1. Open the left sidebar.
2. Click on your profile icon in the top right (or bottom left depending on your client).
3. Navigate to the **Settings** menu.

## 1. Custom Instructions & Memory (The Personalization Tab)
Under the **Personalization** tab, you will find the two most critical tools for Context Engineering.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Memory** allows the AI to automatically remember details about you across different chats. Ensure the toggle is turned ON.
> **Custom Instructions** allow you to explicitly define your identity and how the AI should respond. This is where you establish your "Vibe."

## 2. Higher Intelligence (The General Tab)
Thin Prompting only works if the AI is smart enough to infer what you mean without you spelling it out. 

Navigate to the **General** tab in settings. Look for the toggle labeled **Higher Intelligence** (or similar model routing settings). When enabled, ChatGPT will automatically use a stronger reasoning model when you ask a complex question. This is the engine that powers Adam's Law!

## 3. Temporary Chat
Finally, when you are doing one-off tasks (like translating a sentence or asking a random trivia question) and you *don't* want the AI to remember it or pollute your baseline context, use **Temporary Chat**. You can toggle this on from the model dropdown at the top of a new chat.

With your ChatGPT baseline established, you are ready for the ultimate power-user move: connecting it to a local Digital Twin.
