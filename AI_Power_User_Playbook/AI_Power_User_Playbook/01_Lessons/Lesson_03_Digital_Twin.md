# Lesson 3: The Digital Twin (Obsidian)

Your ChatGPT Custom Instructions and Memory act as your baseline context (configured in [[Lesson_02_The_Baseline]]), but to truly become a power user, you need a "Second Brain" to store your complex theories, structures, and ongoing projects. We will use **Obsidian** for this.

## Why Obsidian?
Obsidian is a highly customizable note-taking app where all your data is stored locally as plain markdown text files. (See [[03_Digital_Twin_Theory]] for why this is necessary).
This is crucial for AI power users because:
1. Your data is future-proof (it isn't locked into a proprietary cloud server).
2. It's incredibly fast.
3. You can easily feed plain markdown text into an LLM.

## Setting Up Your Vault
A "Vault" in Obsidian is simply a folder on your computer. 
1. Download Obsidian from https://obsidian.md/download
2. Create a new folder on your computer named `AI_Power_User_Playbook` (or any name you prefer).
3. Open Obsidian and select **Open folder as vault**, pointing it to the folder you just created.

## Structuring the Vault
To build a functional Digital Twin, you need organization. Create these foundational folders inside your vault:
*   `01_Lessons`: For structured guides and courses.
*   `02_Concepts`: For theoretical deep-dives.
*   `03_Assets`: For screenshots and media.

## The Canvas (ZUI)
One of the most powerful features in Obsidian is the **Canvas**. This provides a Zooming User Interface (ZUI)—an infinite canvas where you can build massive mind maps. 

You can use the Canvas to:
*   Map out complex AI architectures (zooming out to see the overarching workflow, zooming in to see individual prompt strings).
*   Connect different concept notes visually.

By maintaining this Digital Twin, you have a permanent, highly structured context database. When you need the AI to perform a complex task, you don't need to write a thick prompt; you just paste in the relevant notes from your Obsidian vault and use a Thin Prompt to set the goal.
