# Lesson 0: The Primer (Unlearning AI Myths)

Welcome to the AI Power User Playbook. Before we begin learning how to use AI like a power user, we must first unlearn the habits of the past. 

In education, there is a concept called **Via Negativa**—teaching by subtraction or inversion. Just as the fastest way to learn critical thinking is to study logical fallacies (how *not* to think), the fastest way to master AI is to understand "AI Fallacies." (Read more in [[00_Primer_AI_Myths]]).

## The Danger of "Prompt Engineering"
If you learned how to use AI prior to 2024, you likely learned "Prompt Engineering." Because early models (like GPT-3) were easily confused, users developed massive, complex workarounds. They wrote giant paragraphs of constraints, forced the AI to output specific XML formats just to speak, and even used psychological tricks (like promising the AI a $100 tip).

> [!WARNING]
> **Outdated habits are actively harmful.** Modern reasoning models (like GPT-4 and o1) have patched these early vulnerabilities. If you try to force a highly intelligent model into a rigid, complex prompt template today, you constrain its reasoning ability and get a worse result.

## The Unintuitive Reality of Control
The most unintuitive truth about modern AI is this: **giving up control yields better results.**

As humans, when we are dealing with complex problems, our instinct is to micromanage. When using an LLM, trying to micromanage every single step of a process usually causes the AI to trip over your constraints. 

To be a power user, you must embrace the alien nature of the tool. Your job is to:
1. State your goal clearly.
2. Provide the right background context.
3. **Step out of the way.**

In the next lesson, we will explore exactly how to provide that background context so you never have to micromanage the AI again.
