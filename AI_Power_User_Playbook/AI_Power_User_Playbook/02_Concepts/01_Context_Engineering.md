# Part 1: Context Engineering

Context Engineering is the foundational concept of the AI Power User Playbook (taught practically in [[Lesson_01_Context_Engineering]]). 

This framework is derived from the philosophies of **Andrej Karpathy** (Founding member of OpenAI). As Karpathy noted, we are shifting away from "Prompt Engineering" (writing massive, complex, rigid prompts) toward "Context Engineering"—which he describes as *"the delicate art and science of filling the AI's context window with exactly the right background information."*

When your context is engineered properly (using tools like Custom Instructions, Memory, and Obsidian Vaults), you don't need to over-explain yourself in every chat. This enables **Thin Prompting**.

## Core Concepts

### Thin Prompting
Thin prompting is the practice of giving the AI brief, high-level, goal-oriented instructions rather than micromanaging its steps. It relies on a properly engineered background context and the "Higher intelligence" setting to infer intent. This aligns with Karpathy's vision that natural language ("English") is the only programming language you need when interacting with advanced models.

### Adam's Law
**Adam's Law** states that prompts crafted with simple, natural language yield smarter and more effective results from LLMs than overly complex, rigid prompts. By speaking to the AI naturally, you allow its sophisticated internal reasoning engine to take the lead rather than forcing it into a brittle constraint.
