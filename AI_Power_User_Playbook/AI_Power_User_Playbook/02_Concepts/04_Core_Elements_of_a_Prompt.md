# Concept: The 4 Core Elements of a Prompt

To understand why our methodology works, we must deconstruct the conventional approach to interacting with AI. Historically, industry experts taught that a "perfect" prompt must contain four core elements within the chat box. 

Our method of **Context Engineering** completely changes where these elements live, paving the way for **Thin Prompting**.

## The Conventional 4 Elements
In standard Prompt Engineering, a user is taught to include all of the following in every single prompt:
1. **Persona:** Who the AI should act as (e.g., "Act as a senior software engineer...").
2. **Context:** The background information (e.g., "Here is my entire project history...").
3. **Format:** How the output should look (e.g., "Output exactly 3 bullet points, no apologies...").
4. **Task:** What you actually want the AI to do (e.g., "Find the bug in this code").

## The Context Engineering Shift
In our framework, we separate the *Background* from the *Action*. 

1. **Persona** is permanently mapped to **Custom Instructions**. The AI already knows who it is before you type a word.
2. **Context** is handled by the AI's **Memory** or pasted directly from your **Obsidian Digital Twin**.
3. **Format** is either defined in Custom Instructions or naturally inferred by the **Higher Intelligence** reasoning models.
4. **Task** is the only thing left in the chat box.

## The Result: Thin Prompting
When you engineer elements 1, 2, and 3 into the background system, your actual day-to-day prompt is reduced *only* to element 4 (The Task). 

Instead of writing a 500-word "thick prompt" to fix a typo, you simply paste the text and type: *"Fix the typos."* This is the essence of Thin Prompting: relying on a robust engineered context so that your daily interactions remain incredibly lightweight.
