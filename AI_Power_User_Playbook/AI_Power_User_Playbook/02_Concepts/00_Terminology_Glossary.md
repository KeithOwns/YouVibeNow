# Glossary: The Context Engineering Framework

This glossary defines the proprietary terminology used in this methodology and connects it to conventional industry jargon. This serves as the foundational nomenclature for future white papers and academic discussions regarding this optimized AI workflow.

## Proprietary Terms & Conventional Equivalents

### 1. Context Engineering
*   **Definition:** The delicate art and science of filling the AI's context window with exactly the right background information, system instructions, and file structures *before* a task is assigned. 
*   **Authority Citation:** Endorsed by **Andrej Karpathy** in 2025 as the successor to Prompt Engineering.
*   **Conventional Equivalent:** *Prompt Engineering / System Prompting.* (However, Prompt Engineering implies writing massive instructions in the chat box, whereas Context Engineering implies building a permanent background OS).

### 2. Thin Prompting
*   **Definition:** The practice of giving the AI brief, high-level, goal-oriented instructions, relying on the pre-engineered background context and the model's native intelligence to infer intent. Builds on Karpathy's assertion that natural language is the ultimate programming language.
*   **Conventional Equivalent:** *Zero-shot prompting / Vague prompting.* (Conventionally, this is frowned upon, but in our framework, it is the goal).
*   **Opposite Of:** *Thick Prompting / Rich Prompting / Few-shot Prompting.*

### 3. Adam's Law
*   **Definition:** The principle stating that prompts crafted with simple, natural language yield smarter and more effective results from modern LLMs than overly complex, rigid constraints. 
*   **Conventional Equivalent:** *Natural Language Interaction / Goal-directed Prompting.* 

### 4. AI Fallacies (Via Negativa)
*   **Definition:** The specific list of outdated, harmful prompt engineering habits that users must *unlearn* (such as begging the AI, forcing weird XML formats, or over-constraining the model). 
*   **Conventional Equivalent:** *Anti-patterns / Deprecated Practices.*

### 5. Symbiosis (Reasoning Engine vs. Storage Engine)
*   **Definition:** The architectural separation of compute and storage. The LLM (e.g., ChatGPT) acts purely as the dynamic **Reasoning Engine**, while a local markdown structure (e.g., Obsidian) acts as the permanent, organized **Storage Engine**.
*   **Conventional Equivalent:** *Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture / Headless AI integration.*

### 6. Digital Twin
*   **Definition:** Your highly structured, locally hosted Obsidian vault that acts as a perfect replica of your knowledge, projects, and context, ready to be fed into the Reasoning Engine.
*   **Conventional Equivalent:** *Second Brain / Knowledge Graph / Personal Knowledge Management (PKM).*
