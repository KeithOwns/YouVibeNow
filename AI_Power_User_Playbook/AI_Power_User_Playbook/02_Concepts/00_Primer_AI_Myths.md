# Primer: Unlearning AI Myths

The fastest way to learn how to use modern AI is to unlearn the habits of the past. In education, this is known as **Via Negativa** (teaching by subtraction). Just as learning logical fallacies is the best primer for critical thinking, learning "AI Fallacies" is the best primer for AI mastery (taught in [[Lesson_00_Primer]]).

## The Myth of Prompt Engineering
When early LLMs (like GPT-3) were released, they were easily confused. Users had to invent complex workarounds to get good results:
*   Writing massive paragraphs of constraints.
*   Forcing the AI to use specific XML or JSON formats just to parse basic text.
*   Using psychological tricks (e.g., "I will tip you $100 if you do this right").

Because AI models are updated at a blistering pace, these early vulnerabilities have been patched by smarter reasoning engines. Today, these old "best practices" are actively harmful. If you try to force a modern, highly intelligent model into a rigid, complex prompt template, you constrain its reasoning ability and get worse results.

## The Unintuitive Reality
The most unintuitive truth about modern AI is that **giving up control yields better results**. Humans naturally want to micromanage tasks when they are unsure. With AI, trying to micromanage every step of a process usually causes the AI to trip over your constraints. 

To be a power user, you must embrace the alien nature of the tool: state your goal clearly, provide the right context, and step out of the way.
