# Concept: The Baseline Settings

While the actual instructions for changing settings in ChatGPT are straightforward (see [[Lesson_02_The_Baseline]]), understanding *why* these settings matter is crucial for Context Engineering.

## Custom Instructions
Custom Instructions act as your permanent identity layer. They tell the AI *who* it is talking to and *how* it should respond. 

Without Custom Instructions, the AI is a generic assistant that aims for a safe, verbose, middle-of-the-road tone. By setting strong Custom Instructions, you establish the "vibe" (the baseline) of the interaction. You never have to say "Be concise" or "Act like an expert coder" again, because that context is permanently engineered into the system.

## Memory
The Memory feature allows the AI to pick up on your specific quirks, projects, and preferences over time. 

If Custom Instructions are the rigid foundation, Memory is the dynamic layer that evolves. The combination of these two features ensures that the AI is highly attuned to your specific needs, making Thin Prompting incredibly effective.

## Temporary Chat
Why does Temporary Chat matter to a power user? Because you must protect your baseline context!

If you use your main ChatGPT account to ask it to "write a birthday poem for my dog" or "what is the capital of France," the AI might start writing those details into its Memory. This pollutes your carefully engineered context. 

Power users rely on **Temporary Chat** for one-off tasks and random queries so that the interaction remains completely isolated from their main, professional workflow.
